About us

The University of Chicago Applied Math Club (UCAMC) is an undergraduate club which provides a platform for students interested in applications of mathematics and statistics, such as in finance (volatility, derivative markets), economics (price theory and game theory), computer science (quantum computing and data science/machine learning), physics and computational biology. We hold meetings where professors and graduate students present on their work, and where corporations (e.g. hedge funds and cybersecurity firms) discuss the way they use mathematics. We conduct workshops, where experienced students in desired fields teach newer students both fundamental skills for industry (like probability, linear algebra), as well as topics that foster curiosity. We also have a platform for where students can prepare for and succeed on the Putnam competition.
